**[📚 Books]**

- [Technology and the Virtues](https://www.amazon.com/dp/019049851X) by [Shannon Vallor FRSE](https://www.linkedin.com/in/shannon-vallor/) - the book develops a practical framework for seeking a future worth wanting by means of the deliberate cultivation of technomoral virtues: specific skills and strengths of character, adapted to the unique challenges of 21st century life, that offer the human family our best chance of learning to live wisely and well with emerging technologies.

**[📝 Articles/Essays]**

- [The Reverse Information Paradox](https://x.com/satyanadella/article/2076323181154230284) by [Satya Nadella](https://www.linkedin.com/in/satyanadella/) - building on economist Kenneth Arrow's classic information paradox, the piece argues that companies risk quietly leaking their competitive edge (unique data, processes, and institutional knowledge) into the infrastructure of the AI vendors they pay, unless they deliberately architect ways to retain ownership of what they learn (evals, memories, workflows) rather than letting it all compound inside someone else's platform.

- [A Framework for Engineering Agentic Value](https://underline.vc/engineering-agentic-value) - in this article, I explore whether (and how) the value of an agentic AI system can be adequately defined in terms of both performance and economic sustainability, and I propose a framework for doing so.
